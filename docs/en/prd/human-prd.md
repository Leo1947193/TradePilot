# Context
I am an American stock exchange beginner, my workflow to buy and sell a stock is like this:
1. Serf on Rednote to see current hot spots and events that might influence the market
2. Choose a stock that I **subjectively** think can bring me profits.
3. Do some basic technical analysis: just analyze the candlestick chart, macd, rsi, and think if the price will increase or decrease.

# My Problems
Individual investors commonly face the following challenges when making stock purchase decisions:

1. **Lack of a structured analysis workflow**

    Investment decisions are often driven by subjective judgment rather than a systematic evaluation of both fundamental factors (e.g., financials, industry context) and technical indicators (e.g., trends, volume, support/resistance levels).

2. **Fragmented information and lack of synthesis**

    Existing tools (e.g., market data platforms and charting tools) primarily provide raw data and indicators, but do not effectively synthesize information into actionable insights. Users are left to manually interpret and combine disparate signals.

3. **Lack of objective decision support**

    Investors struggle to consolidate multi-dimensional signals—such as fundamentals, technicals, and market sentiment—into a unified, comparable, and objective basis for decision-making.


**Therefore**, there is a need for a stock decision-support system that automates analysis and delivers structured outputs to:

- Reduce analysis time and effort

- Improve objectivity in decision-making

- Enable consistent review and iterative improvement of investment strategies
